About South Queensferry

My home town South Queensferry is a small sea side town located to the west side of Edinburgh . "The Ferry" is famousley known for the 3 bridges that cross the river Forth: The Forth Bridge, The Forth Road Bridge and The Queensferry Crossing.

Facts

  • The anuual "ferry fair" started in the 1930s
  • St Mary's Episcopal Church, also known as the Priory Church is the town's oldest building built in the 1450s
  • Queensferry has three primary schools, Echline Primary, Queensferry Primary and St. Margarets Primary and one secondary school Queensferry High School.

Burry Man

The burry man is a local man that is covered head to toe in sticky burs only leaving his shoes, hand and two eye holes exposed. On the top layer he wears a sash, flowers and a floral hat and he holds two staves. His ability to bend his arms or sit down is very restricted during the long day and his progress is a slow walk with frequent pauses. Two locals in ordinary clothes assist him throughout the process, helping him hold the staves, guiding his route whilst enthusiastic children go from door-to-door collecting money on his behalf.
